Global Plastic Production (1950-2015)

In Year 2015, the Global Plastic Production has reached 381 million, tonnes which is the highest over the past 65 years.

The production has increased significantly over the years. As there is only 2 million tonnes of production in Year 1950.

There is a decrease in production in 2008 and 2009 which is most probably due to global financial crisis.

How Much Plastic Waste Is Produced?

Kuwait, Guyana, Germany, Netherlands, Ireland, United States of America generated high number of plastic waste.

Countries with high number of plastic waste most probably has large number of population.

Does GDP Per Capita Affect How Much Plastic Waste Being Produced?

According to the chart, GDP Per Capita does affect the Production of Plastic Waste Being Produced. As the GDP per Capita increase, the plastic waste generation per person will also increase. A possible factor is that in country with high GDP per Capita, they are have better economic development and hence the production and usage of plastic increases.

How Plastic Being Disposed?

The global plastic waste disposal method has changed over time. There are 3 ways to dispose the plastic waste which is Discarded, Incinerated and Recycled from 1980 to 2015. In 1980, the plastic waste was 100% being discarded.

The percentage of disposal method for Incineration and Recycling slowly increased from Year 1981 and 1987 onwards. If the disposal of plastic waste follows the trend of this graph, the Incineration rate and Recycling rate will increase over the years while the Discarding rate will decrease.
